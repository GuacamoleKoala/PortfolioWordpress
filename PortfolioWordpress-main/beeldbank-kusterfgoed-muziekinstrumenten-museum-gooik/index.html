During my postgraduate in cultural heritage: track digitalisation I worked out a professional digitalisation plan for the museum of musical instruments De Cam in Gooik (Belgium).

The museum consists of a private collection of folk music instruments. I first develop a strategy to create standardized metadata for the instruments on the basis of Belgian and European standards (Mim [Brussels], Mimo [Europe]). I also briefly touched on how to approach the creation of 3D-models for some of the top pieces of the collection. All of this was done with the idea of using open source software.

Writings

In the paper below I showed how through musicological classification (Hornbostel and Sachs) several groups of instruments can be created for the creation of metadata and a digitalisation strategy. These groups are subject to different photographical methods to capture them as a set of 2D images. For exceptional pieces I developed a strategy to capture and create 3D models by looking into photographical methods of photogrammetry. The digitalisation strategy can be read in full down below.
